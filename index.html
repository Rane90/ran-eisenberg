About

I am a PhD researcher at Bar-Ilan University, advised by Prof. Ofir Lindenbaum. My research focuses on multimodal alignment and representation learning — building models that discover structure across heterogeneous data modalities without heavy supervision.

My work spans self-supervised multi-view clustering, canonical correlation-based time-series alignment, interpretable concept-guided classification, and deep learning for bioinformatics (immune receptor sequence alignment).

I also serve as a Data Scientist at the Bar-Ilan Data Science Institute, leading end-to-end ML projects (LLMs, NLP, computer vision) in healthcare and large-scale historical data analysis, in collaboration with SHEBA Hospital, Yad Vashem, and Paamonim.

Previously I interned at Microsoft Surface applying computer vision to sensor data. I teach Intro to Deep Learning and Data Science with Python (lecturer from 2025), and host the BIU Learning Club seminar series.
