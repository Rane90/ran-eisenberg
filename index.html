About

I am a PhD researcher at Bar-Ilan University, advised by Prof. Ofir Lindenbaum. My research focuses on multimodal alignment and representation learning. I hold an MSc in Computer Science from Ben-Gurion University and a BA in Psychology & Cognitive Science.

My work spans over multi-view clustering, time-series alignment, permutation learning, interpretability, and deep learning for bioinformatics (i.e, sequence alignment).

I also work as a Data Scientist at the Bar-Ilan Data Science Institute (DSI), on ML projects (LLMs/NLP, CV) in healthcare and large-scale historical data analysis, in collaboration with organizations like SHEBA Hospital, Yad Vashem, and Paamonim.

I host the BIU Learning Club seminar.
